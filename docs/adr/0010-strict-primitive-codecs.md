# Strict primitive codecs, booleans as 1/0

Built-in codecs never coerce: a decode either yields the type's canonical form or fails with a DecodeError carrying expected/actual detail. Booleans encode as `1`/`0` (the format every deliberately designed client — go-redis, redis-rs — writes; zio-redis's `true`/`false` comes from `toString` and cannot round-trip) and decoding accepts only those two forms, so a foreign writer storing `"true"` surfaces as an error instead of being silently absorbed (go-redis reads anything but `"1"` as false). The String codec rejects invalid UTF-8 rather than substituting replacement chars, because reading binary data through the String codec is the most likely codec bug a user will write and mojibake hides it. Numerics are the one place stdlib leniency is accepted (`toDoubleOption` parses `"Infinity"`, whitespace, suffixes): our own encode is always canonical, and rejecting near-miss foreign numbers helps no diagnosis. The encode formats are one-way doors — they persist in users' servers — which is why they are recorded here.
